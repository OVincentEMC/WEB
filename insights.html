My Journey into Programming & Game Development

When I first dove into programming, it felt like jumping into the deep end. I started with C++, and let’s just say—it was a lot! The syntax was foreign, and the errors? Endless. But as time went on, I started realizing something important: programming isn’t just about writing code. It’s about solving problems and creating things that didn’t exist before. That realization made everything click, and I started to fall in love with the process.


Fast forward to today, and my main goal is to become a game developer. I’m still in the early stages, but I’ve been diving into tools like Unity and Unreal Engine, and I’m hooked. The idea of creating worlds—interactive ones where players can explore, solve puzzles, or experience new stories—is what keeps me going. It’s such an exciting space to be in.


What inspires me most are the games I’ve played. Each one, whether big or small, has taught me something valuable—whether it’s in game design, storytelling, or how to make people feel something. These experiences fuel my desire to push my own boundaries, to create something that resonates with people. That’s what I want my games to do: leave an impression, make someone smile, or even make them think.


But, man, the journey isn’t always easy. There are bugs, late nights, and times when you wonder if the code will ever work. But those moments are part of the process, and honestly, they’re some of the most rewarding. Each challenge is a chance to grow, and there’s always something new to learn along the way. That’s what I love about this field—it’s never the same, and I’m always getting better.


One thing I’ve learned is that storytelling in games is so powerful. Whether it’s a huge narrative or a simple, emotional moment, games let you experience stories in such a unique way. I want my games to tell stories that matter, whether it’s through the world design, the characters, or how players interact with the environment. It’s about connecting with the player on a deeper level.


Another huge takeaway has been how collaborative game development can be. While I’m working solo on a lot of projects right now, I know the best games are made by teams. In the future, I’m excited to work with artists, designers, sound engineers—people with different skills who can bring something new to the table. Teamwork is a huge part of creating something truly special.


And inspiration can come from anywhere, really. A conversation with a friend, a random thought, or even a cool story I read somewhere. I try to stay open to all kinds of ideas because sometimes the best game mechanic or character comes from the most unexpected places. I’ve learned to keep my mind open and my notebook ready!


Ultimately, I want to create games that not only entertain but inspire others to follow their own creative passions. I hope my work can encourage someone to take a leap into game development or any other creative field. There’s something so fulfilling about bringing an idea to life and sharing it with others, and I want to keep doing that as long as I can.


The road ahead is long, but I’m excited for what’s to come. Every project is a chance to learn, grow, and improve. I’m looking forward to the day when I can share my games with the world, and maybe, just maybe, make an impact in someone’s life the way games have done for me. It’s a journey worth taking, and I wouldn’t trade it for anything.
